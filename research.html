Research

My current research focuses on the accurate emulation of brain functions in the fruit fly Drosophila melanogaster. A major element of this work is the development of Neurokernel, a scalable parallel software framework for running this emulation on multiple Graphics Processing Units (GPUs). Neurokernel will also serve as a platform for other ongoing efforts at my advisor's research group to shed light on how the brain processes information by employing analytic and computational approaches grounded in fundamental results from digital and analog signal processing.

A brief description of the Neurokernel project can be found here. We are in the processing of preparing more extensive information regarding the project for release during the Summer of 2013.

Software written by myself and my colleagues at the Bionet Group as part of our research activities may be downloaded from our group's GitHub repository. Other open source software and useful code snippets that I have written are respectively available on my GitHub repository and gist pages.
